ABOUT COSMOPOLITA
A HYPERMODERNIST NOVEL BY NATHAN SAINT-YVES

Her Comforting Light is a novel exploring the intersections of bureaucracy, identity, and cosmic wonder in a world of fragmented sovereignties. Written by Nathan Saint-Yves, it follows the journey of Elzbieta Vorn through the labyrinthine port city of Port-Actual, where administrative procedures reveal deeper truths about human connection and the nature of belonging.

The narrative weaves together encyclopedic detail with intimate personal stories, examining how systems of governance shape our understanding of self and other. Through Elzbieta's eyes, we encounter a city that is both a physical space and a metaphysical process, a place where arrivals are not just geographical but existential.

This work is part of an ongoing exploration of speculative fiction that takes bureaucracy seriously—not as a punchline, but as a fundamental aspect of how we organize our worlds and ourselves.

About the Author

Nathan Saint-Yves is a writer and researcher interested in the poetics of administration, the aesthetics of infrastructure, and the speculative possibilities of everyday systems. His work spans fiction, criticism, and experimental forms that blend narrative with documentary impulses.

When not writing, he can be found contemplating the cosmic womb or arguing with paperwork.

BIO
Biography

Erin Karennso was not born so much as she was deposited — the particular verb Sorekin would have used, and which she herself preferred. The city of Port-Actual had a way of generating people out of its own administrative processes, producing individuals whose paperwork preceded their personalities by several years, and Erin was, in this sense, a native. Her earliest documented existence was not a birth certificate but a Category Three-Gamma provisional presence form, issued to her mother seventeen months before Erin herself arrived — an administrative error that the Bureau of Ongoing Determination had declined to correct on the grounds that the correction would generate more paperwork than the original error.

She had grown up in the Littoral District, in one of the corrugated residential blocks that lined the inner harbour, her childhood organized around two fixed points: the smell of brine through single-pane windows, and the certainty that whatever category she was, it was the wrong one. Her mother, a licensed fixer of some local reputation, had impressed upon her from an early age the importance of documentation, the danger of categories, and the particular professional virtue of knowing exactly which rules were worth breaking and in what order. Erin had absorbed the first two lessons thoroughly and developed her own conclusions about the third.

§ 2
Formation

By seventeen she had been arrested twice, both times for infractions the Port Authority's own legal counsel had subsequently admitted were "procedurally ambiguous." By twenty she was working semi-officially for a Compact sub-body that required people who understood ambiguity as a professional condition rather than an obstacle. The work was unspecified in its particulars, well-compensated in its generalities, and entirely off the record in the sense that the record itself was classified. She kept her hair pink because it was the one categorically legible thing about her, and she kept the gun because Port-Actual was Port-Actual.

Her relationship to Elzbieta Vorn is established gradually across Book II and does not resolve in the way either of them intend. This is, the editor's notes suggest, the point. Erin arrives in the narrative the way she arrives everywhere: through a side door, holding something that may or may not be a weapon, wearing something that is definitely not appropriate for the weather, and already three steps ahead of whatever the current situation technically requires.

§ 3
Affiliations & Status

Erin's formal affiliations are contested in both the novel's diegesis and its apparatus. She appears on four separate registries under three separate names and two separate biometric profiles, a situation the Semiperipheral Registry Bureau describes, in a footnote to a footnote, as "technically impossible but apparently the case." Her current documentation status is Category PROVISIONAL UNRESOLVED (CLASS INDETERMINATE), a category which, the editor notes, was created specifically to accommodate her.
